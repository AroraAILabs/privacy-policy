How Your Data is Stored

Reminders is an offline-first app. All your reminder data (titles, schedules, notes, preferences) is stored locally on your device. We have no backend servers and do not collect or store any of your data remotely.

The only external services used are optional Google integrations (Drive backup and Gmail delivery), which store data in your own Google account — not on our servers.

How to Delete Your Data

Step 1: Sign Out of Google (if signed in)

Open the app → Settings → Sign Out. This removes your Google OAuth token and email address from the device.

Step 2: Uninstall the App

Uninstalling Reminders permanently deletes all locally stored data, including:

  • All reminders, schedules, and notification triggers
  • App preferences and settings
  • Encrypted credentials stored in EncryptedSharedPreferences
  • Any cached or queued data

Step 3: Delete Google Drive Backups (if applicable)

If you used the optional cloud backup feature, your backup files are stored in your own Google Drive. To delete them:

  1. Open Google Drive
  2. Search for "Reminders Backup"
  3. Delete the backup files
  4. Empty your Google Drive trash

What Gets Deleted

  • All local data — reminders, triggers, preferences, encrypted tokens — deleted when you uninstall the app
  • Google OAuth token — removed when you sign out from Settings
  • Google Drive backups — stored in your own Drive account; you can delete them directly from Google Drive

We retain no data on any server. Once you uninstall the app, there is nothing left on our side to delete.
